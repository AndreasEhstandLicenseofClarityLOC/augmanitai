## How to Become a Maintainer

See the "Becoming a Maintainer" section of `GOVERNANCE.md`. In short: sustained, high-quality contributions over a minimum of 6 months, demonstrated understanding of ISO 704/1087/30042 terminology principles, and a nomination from an existing maintainer that is not blocked within the consensus period.

## Maintainer Communication

- Public: GitHub Discussions, Issues, and Pull Requests
- Private (security, CoC violations, personnel): see `SECURITY.md`
- Async by default; no required meetings

## Trust and Responsibility

Maintainers are expected to:

1. Uphold the Code of Conduct (`CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`)
2. Respond to pull requests and issues in their domain within a reasonable time
3. Never merge their own non-trivial changes without second review once the team is larger than 2
4. Be transparent about conflicts of interest when reviewing proposals
5. Sign commits and releases (GPG or Sigstore) for supply-chain integrity
